ASSIST 2.0

The purpose of this project is to continue to develop, implement and evaluate a unique reablement service, ASSIST 2.0. ASSIST 2.0 is designed based on the research group's knowledge regarding reablement including a feasibility study testing ASSIST 1.0. This study will guide the present project as well as a scoping review about rehabilitation at home that is currently undertaken.

The project will determine the differences in the reablement service (ASSIST 2.0) compared to ordinary homecare regarding older adults’ performance in everyday activities, self-efficacy, perceived health, and quality of life. ASSIST integrates education and support from health professionals to homecare staff and uses technological solutions to facilitate communication between all stakeholders. In the ASSIST 2.0 intervention, older adults will identify problems with performing needed and wanted everyday activities. Homecare staff, under the guidance of health professionals, will support the older adults to perform these activities. Homecare staff will receive education and coaching from the professionals to assure adherence to the reablement approach.

The Medical Research Council’s (MRC) guidelines for evaluating complex interventions and the CONSORT statement for non-pharmacological trials will continue to be implemented in this project.

The research question addressed in project B is: Can older adults that receive ASSIST 2.0 increase their performance and satisfaction in everyday life activities, perceive a higher level of self-efficacy, perceived health, and quality of life?

Study design and research setting

The project includes an evaluation of the effects of ASSIST 2.0 in comparison with ordinary homecare services. A cluster randomised controlled trial (RCT) with recruitment through two homecare units in Stockholm and two homecare units in Uppsala randomised to an Intervention group (IG) delivering ASSIST 2.0 or to the Control group (CG) delivering ordinary homecare services. The ASSIST 2.0 program includes health professionals (registered nurses, occupational therapists, and physiotherapists) working at the intervention sites and who will be responsible for the education and coaching of the homecare staff. To do this, the health professionals working at the sites delivering ASSIST (IG) will participate in a training program provided by the researchers through various workshops.

Intervention and implementation of the program

The reablement program ASSIST 2.0 includes the following parts: a) an intervention directed to the older adults so they can perform their everyday activities that they need and want to do. This will be achieved by optimal support from the homecare staff; b) education for the health professionals in the art and science of reablement provided by the researchers; c) education and support via mentoring and coaching sessions held by the health professionals for the homecare staff ensuring the correct provision of reablement and d) support for communication between the older adults, their significant others, the homecare staff, and the health professionals, by integrating QR codes into the service.

Methods of data collection and analysis

To study the effects of ASSIST 2.0 in the IG in comparison with a CG, 90 older adults living at home and in need of homecare services and their possible significant others will be recruited. A power calculation is based on our feasibility study. The outcome measure Canadian Occupational Performance Measure showed that for a 95% chance of demonstrating a real treatment effect (95% power) 45 older persons will be included in each group with a calculation of dropouts. Both quantitative and qualitative methods will be used to evaluate the intervention. Standardized quantitative instruments will be used to ascertain the older adults, a) perceived performance and satisfaction in activities in everyday life, b) independence in Activities of Daily Living (ADL), c) amount of sedentary behaviour, d) self-efficacy, and e) general life quality. For the significant others a) caregiver burden and b) provision of informal care will be assessed. For both the older adults and significant others a) perceived life satisfaction and b) level of anxiety and depression will be assessed. The experiences of the intervention will be explored through qualitative interviews with the participating older adults, their significant others, healthcare, and homecare staff.
